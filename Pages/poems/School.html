School

This poem talks about very sensitive subjects and could be triggering for some. Take care of yourselves.

Trigger Warnings // Depression & Suicide

My heart.
Dipped in chocolate.
Ripped apart.
I remember walking down the halls
I remember the start.
It was a fairy tale, wishing well.
Called it spell, more like a curse.
With every ring it was cast.
Memories overlap and they last.
There's never no such thing as "remembering my past." This is my future.
I live it.
Eat, sleep, breath, repeat it
Don't know if I'd feel it...
You know?
When I am pinched.
Always told that's the best way to wake up from a dream. But it's not.
The only thing pinching does is leave scars from the times I tried to wake up and failed. The white chalk, black marker, red ink. Let this sink in.
The desk, the chairs, the fights, the dares, the pressure, the stairs. The things I try to bare with little or no care and you want me to try harder? Pick up the pin more?
Read more?
You think staring at words that only make me crawl into an abyss won't make me run out the door? You want me to care more?
You don't give two shits about me and you tell me to keep my face up off the floor? What gives you the right?
As a teacher you are to guide me not thrive off me. Not make the class you past, another thing to type down. I am surrounded around people who tell me I am either going to swim or sink but they never accounted for the one kid who wanted to drowned. Who wanted to die.
You label me not working as laziness yet in my mind I am the craziest. I sleep in class trying to create my happiness. Fuck school I am bad at this.
I've had this shit.
I cry at my desk cause I want to fuck this shit. Kids dropping pins out the window and I am just thinking if I jump will I die from this. I can't blame my teachers, they weren't prepared for this. All most declared forfeit.
All these test and numbers and I can't see for shit.

I am done with it.
My papers are loose, my tie is my noose, the uniform is my tux, the room is my coffin, and so so much this happens way too often. The day starts my clock, the one I stair at waiting for the end of class the shattered glass I walk on to make it to the next. The hexadecimal number of times I blew things out of proportion I am a pyrotechnical calling all my feelings making them comprehensible as an unexceptional human being the forgettable beams people call my brightness yet they don't mean it by the slightest I am not to be looked to.
Who would want to.
My intake of life is swalloed with a knife something sharp to make this go down easy I think I feel quezy
