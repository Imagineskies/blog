Dear Future Self

This poem is one of my lighter, more hopeful ones. I wouldn't say there are any trigger warnings needed for this one.

Dear future self,
How do you live?
I ask you this because when you were a kid you believed every saying by the slitest. In your mind you were just a stastic.
A number in an aray of people.
Yet, you are so much more.
You would use this to go to war with yourself on every decision you make. When I ask "How do you live?", i'm asking whether or not you're scared to go outside by yourself, or if you're able to walk where you want, when you want.

Dear future self,
Where do you live?
I ask this because when you where young you believed that you would never leave Detroit. Yet, i believe you do.
I believe you can do anything you put your mind to. That you are alot stronger than you think. You were on the brink of Death, but insted, you became something you never thought you would. You should continue on with yourself to prove to the world that you aren't some stastic.

Dear future self,
Stop looking at your scars as shameful parts of your past. Look at them as the battle scars that show the proof that you went to hell, and survived. Inside you are a flower just trying to survive, and you are going to have thoes weeds that follow around.
